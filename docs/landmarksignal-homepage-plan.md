# LandmarkSignal Homepage Plan

## 1. Recommended Site Architecture

### V1 Recommendation
V1 should be a single strong homepage, not a broader multi-page marketing site.

This is the right starting point because the immediate buyer job is evaluation, not exploration across a large content surface. A focused homepage can establish credibility, clarify the company-platform-product relationship, and drive a briefing or demo request without the overhead of inventing weak secondary pages before the core story is settled.

### Recommended Top-Level Navigation
- Platform
- Products
- Use Cases
- Company
- Request a Briefing

Navigation notes:
- Keep navigation short and executive-readable.
- Avoid bloated SaaS nav patterns with many dropdowns in v1.
- `Request a Briefing` should be the primary CTA in the header.
- A secondary text CTA such as `View Runtime Overview` can appear in the hero or nearby.

### Recommended Future Expansion After V1
After the homepage is approved, expand into a small, high-signal multi-page site:

- `/platform`
  Purpose: explain LandmarkSignal Runtime in more operational and architectural detail
- `/products`
  Purpose: introduce the product family and how each offering relates to Runtime
- `/use-cases`
  Purpose: map offerings to secure AI operations, governance, private AI, auditability, and decision support
- `/company`
  Purpose: explain company positioning, leadership posture, and mission without startup theater
- `/briefing` or `/contact`
  Purpose: focused conversion page for executive conversations and demos

Optional later additions:
- `/security` or `/trust`
  Purpose: trust posture, deployment principles, governance philosophy, and assurance materials
- `/insights`
  Purpose: publish serious thought leadership once the narrative is established

## 2. Homepage Section Blueprint

### 1. Header
Purpose:
Establish immediate trust, orientation, and a clear path to conversion.

Needs to communicate:
- LandmarkSignal is a serious enterprise company
- The information architecture is disciplined
- A briefing or demo is available without aggressive sales energy

### 2. Hero
Purpose:
State what LandmarkSignal is in one clear, high-trust frame and anchor the homepage around enterprise relevance.

Needs to communicate:
- LandmarkSignal is a trusted intelligence platform company
- The company is focused on secure AI operations and trusted decision support
- The offering is enterprise-grade and relevant to governance-heavy environments
- The next step is a briefing, conversation, or demo

### 3. Platform Clarification Section
Purpose:
Explain the relationship between LandmarkSignal, LandmarkSignal Runtime, and the broader product family.

Needs to communicate:
- LandmarkSignal is the company and platform brand
- LandmarkSignal Runtime is the foundational runtime or operating layer
- The product family sits on or extends from Runtime
- Buyers can understand the structure quickly without jargon

### 4. Why It Matters Section
Purpose:
Frame the operational and governance problem space in enterprise language.

Needs to communicate:
- Secure AI adoption requires governance, auditability, control, and deployment discipline
- LandmarkSignal is built for real operating environments, not generic AI experimentation
- The company understands how security, compliance, risk, and technical leadership evaluate these systems

### 5. Capabilities / Pillars Section
Purpose:
Present the core areas of value in a restrained, structured format.

Needs to communicate:
- Secure AI operations
- Governance and policy control
- Private or local AI deployment support
- Auditability and trusted decision support

This section should avoid hype language and avoid fake product screenshots.

### 6. Product Family Overview
Purpose:
Show how the offering is organized without requiring a separate page to understand the basics.

Needs to communicate:
- There is a coherent product family
- Each product has a defined role
- The family is connected by Runtime rather than feeling like unrelated features

### 7. Use Case Alignment
Purpose:
Help different enterprise stakeholders see themselves in the offering.

Needs to communicate:
- Relevance to CISOs, CIOs, CTOs, compliance leaders, risk leaders, and technical buyers
- Shared platform value with role-specific reasons to care
- Executive usefulness without oversimplifying technical depth

### 8. Company / Trust Posture Section
Purpose:
Reinforce seriousness and decision-worthiness without fake social proof.

Needs to communicate:
- The company is disciplined and credible
- The posture is high-trust, enterprise-oriented, and governance-aware
- Buyers should feel comfortable taking an executive conversation

This can use concise statements about operating principles rather than invented claims.

### 9. Final CTA Section
Purpose:
Convert interest into a concrete next step.

Needs to communicate:
- The appropriate next action is a briefing, conversation, or demo
- This is a high-consideration enterprise process, not a self-serve signup
- The invitation is calm and confident, not pushy

### 10. Footer
Purpose:
Close with clarity and legitimacy.

Needs to communicate:
- Core navigation access
- Company identity
- Contact or briefing path

## 4. Messaging Hierarchy

### Headline Options
1. Trusted intelligence for secure AI operations
2. Secure AI systems built for governed environments
3. Enterprise intelligence for controlled AI adoption
4. The trusted operating layer for enterprise AI
5. Governed AI operations for high-trust organizations

### Subheadline Options
1. LandmarkSignal helps enterprises operationalize AI with the control, auditability, and deployment discipline required in security-conscious environments.
2. Built for organizations where governance, private deployment, and trusted decision support are not optional.
3. LandmarkSignal brings secure AI operations, policy-aware governance, and enterprise-grade runtime control into one disciplined platform approach.
4. For security, risk, compliance, and technical leaders evaluating serious AI adoption, LandmarkSignal provides a clearer path to controlled implementation.
5. From private or local AI deployment to auditable decision support, LandmarkSignal is designed for environments that demand precision and trust.

### Recommended Relationship Between LandmarkSignal, Runtime, and the Product Family
Use a simple parent-platform-product model:

- `LandmarkSignal` is the company and overarching platform identity.
- `LandmarkSignal Runtime` is the core runtime layer that enables secure, governed, enterprise AI operation.
- The `product family` consists of distinct solutions or modules built on Runtime for specific operational and decision-support needs.

Recommended editorial rule:
- Lead with `LandmarkSignal` in high-level brand messaging.
- Introduce `Runtime` early as the foundational layer.
- Describe the product family only after Runtime is understood.

Avoid:
- Presenting Runtime and LandmarkSignal as competing brands
- Naming too many products too early
- Leading with a product matrix before buyers understand the platform story

### CTA Wording Recommendations
Primary CTA options:
- Request a Briefing
- Schedule a Conversation
- Request a Demo

Secondary CTA options:
- Explore Runtime
- View Platform Overview
- See How It Fits

Recommendation:
- Use `Request a Briefing` as the primary CTA because it matches executive evaluation behavior and signals seriousness.
- Use `Explore Runtime` as the secondary CTA if the platform explanation is strong enough to support it.

## 5. Visual System Direction

### Typography Guidance
- Use a sharp, disciplined display face paired with a highly readable body face.
- Favor typographic authority over decorative styling.
- Keep headline language compact and well-spaced.
- Use strong contrast between headline, section titles, and body text.
- Avoid trendy serif softness or playful startup typography.

Recommended typographic mood:
- premium
- exacting
- editorial-technical

### Spacing Rhythm
- Use a restrained but generous rhythm with clear separation between major narrative bands.
- Favor calm vertical pacing over dense SaaS compression.
- Keep internal section spacing consistent enough to feel systemized, but vary it enough to preserve hierarchy.
- Let major sections breathe, especially the hero, platform relationship section, and CTA close.

### Color Direction
- Dark-leaning premium palette
- Charcoal, graphite, slate, and muted steel tones
- Low-chroma cool accents used sparingly for emphasis
- Strong text contrast without stark white-on-black harshness

Accent behavior:
- Use accent color for small emphasis, active states, and key dividers in hierarchy
- Keep the palette quiet and disciplined

### UI Tone
- Calm
- Authoritative
- Precise
- Premium
- Readable
- Restrained

The page should feel like a serious intelligence platform company speaking to executive and technical leadership, not a growth-stage startup trying to manufacture urgency.

### What to Avoid
- Neon or glowing accents
- Purple/cyan AI gradients
- Glassmorphism
- Fake dashboards
- Generic icon-card grids repeated section after section
- Oversized animated hero illustrations
- Inflated claims unsupported by real proof
- Consumer-AI friendliness or playful toy-like UI cues

## 6. Component Guidance

### Reusable Components That Make Sense
- Header and navigation shell
- Section wrapper with consistent width and spacing tokens
- Editorial content block for heading, label, body, and CTA
- Capability tile or pillar block
- Product summary card or row
- Role-based use case block
- CTA band
- Footer

### Where Restrained Component Patterns Could Help
- Platform relationship section:
  A clean diagram or structured tiered layout can clarify the company-runtime-product relationship
- Capability pillars:
  A repeatable modular layout helps comparison and scanning
- Use case alignment:
  Reusable stakeholder blocks can keep role-based messaging consistent
- Final CTA:
  A strong but understated conversion pattern helps the page close with confidence

### Component Patterns That Would Be Too Flashy or Off-Brand
- Carousel-based hero content
- Animated metric counters
- Floating glass cards
- Decorative dashboard mockups with fake data
- Testimonial sliders
- Excessive hover theatrics
- Card stacks with glowing borders
- Over-fragmented chip clouds and badge walls

## 7. Build Recommendation

### What Should Be Built First
1. Information architecture and homepage narrative
2. Messaging hierarchy for hero, platform explanation, and core capability framing
3. Low-fidelity layout and spacing system
4. Visual system foundations: type, color, surfaces, buttons, and section patterns
5. Final homepage composition once messaging and structure are approved

### What Should Wait Until After the Homepage Is Approved
- Secondary page builds
- Detailed product pages
- Expanded use-case pages
- Advanced motion treatments
- Illustration systems beyond subtle abstract support
- CMS or content architecture decisions
- Detailed conversion flow tuning
- Trust-center or security-detail pages unless required for launch

## Planning Summary

The strongest v1 is a single disciplined homepage that behaves like an executive briefing surface rather than a broad marketing site. It should first clarify what LandmarkSignal is, then explain Runtime as the foundational layer, then show how the product family and enterprise use cases fit together, all inside a calm, premium, dark-leaning presentation that prioritizes readability and trust over visual performance.
