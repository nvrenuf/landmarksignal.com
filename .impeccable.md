## Design Context

### Users
LandmarkSignal.com serves enterprise and mid-market decision makers evaluating secure AI adoption, especially CISOs, CIOs, CTOs, security leaders, compliance leaders, risk leaders, and technical buyers.

They typically arrive on the homepage to determine whether LandmarkSignal is a serious company worth speaking with, to understand what the company does, to assess whether the platform is relevant to secure AI operations, governance, private or local AI, auditability, and trusted decision support, and to decide whether the company is credible enough for an executive conversation, briefing, or early demo.

The homepage should help them quickly understand what LandmarkSignal is, clarify the relationship between the company, the Runtime platform, and the product family, reinforce that the offering is enterprise-grade and high-trust, and drive requests for a briefing, conversation, or demo.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel calm, authoritative, and precise.

The interface should evoke trust, clarity, control, seriousness, and confidence. It should communicate discipline, credibility, and fitness for environments where governance, auditability, and security matter.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction should be dark-leaning, premium, restrained, and enterprise-focused. Use dark neutrals with muted slate, charcoal, and graphite tones, with subtle cool accents only where they improve hierarchy or clarity. Readability and composure take priority over visual spectacle.

Reference qualities to draw from:
- Palantir: strategic seriousness, restrained confidence, and an intelligence-platform posture
- Microsoft Security: clear information architecture, readable structure, and polished enterprise presentation
- Vercel: disciplined spacing, sharp typography, and modern minimal confidence

Avoid cyberpunk aesthetics, neon hacker visuals, glowing dark UI, purple/cyan AI palettes, glassmorphism, startup gimmicks, fake dashboards, fake logos, fake testimonials, loud gradients, excessive hero animation, repeated generic SaaS icon-card grids, and any tone that feels like a consumer AI toy, Web3 landing page, or flashy security startup demo.

### Design Principles
- Lead with trust and comprehension: every section should help enterprise buyers understand the company, platform, and product relationship with minimal cognitive load.
- Use premium restraint: strong typography, disciplined spacing, and confident hierarchy should do more work than effects, decoration, or motion.
- Design for executive scrutiny: the page should feel credible in front of security, compliance, risk, and technical leadership audiences.
- Keep motion non-essential: animation should be subtle, sparse, and fully respectful of reduced-motion preferences.
- Prioritize accessibility and readability: maintain strong contrast, legible type, and scan-friendly layouts over visual novelty.
